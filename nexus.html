Before you pay the token

7 things every resale buyer must verify first.

Most buyers discover document problems after paying token money. These checks happen before — so you commit only when the property is clean.

01

Ownership chain

Title search going back 30 years — confirming the seller is the legitimate owner with no ongoing dispute.

02

Encumbrance certificate

Checks whether the property carries any registered mortgages, liabilities, or legal claims that don't show up on the sale deed.

03

Khata status

Verifies that the property is correctly listed with BBMP or the local body — and that the khata is in the seller's name.

04

Outstanding bank loans

Confirms that no active home loan or bank lien is attached to the property that must be cleared before transfer.

05

Sanctioned plan

Checks that the building plan is approved by the relevant authority and that the construction matches the sanctioned layout.

06

Property tax dues

Verifies all tax payments are up to date — so you don't inherit years of unpaid dues when the property changes hands.

07

Sale deed review

The final deed is checked for accurate details — owner names, schedule of property, measurements, and registered encumbrances.
